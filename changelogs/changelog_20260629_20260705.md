## Q: AV24LXJ's EP-vs-GVM briefing chart shows a strange low data band below 1 kWh/km that the other two AV24 trucks don't have — find out why, then add a guard and regenerate the three PDFs

**Diagnosis** (data-confirmed, an INDEPENDENT path from the generator): the briefing
EP-vs-GVM scatter plots **every drive leg** (Outbound + Return + In Transit + …). The
low band is a **SOC-quantisation artefact on short trips**, not a real efficiency or a
segmentation bug. The report defines `Energy Change (kWh) = SOC change % × battery
capacity`, and SOC is reported only to **integer 1 %**. A short (~4 km) laden leg whose
true SOC drop is ~1.5–1.8 % gets quantised to **exactly −1 %**, pinning energy at
`1 % × ~300 kWh ≈ 3 kWh` → EP ≈ 3/4 ≈ **0.7–0.8 kWh/km** (true laden EP ~1.3–1.9). One
1 % step carries ±0.5 % → **±50 % relative energy error**. All band legs share the
identical signature (SOC −1 %, energy ≈ −3 kWh, dist 3.7–6.8 km, EP < 1.5).

**Why only AV24LXJ looked anomalous**: all three trucks have these 1 %-SOC short legs
(LXJ 923 / LXK 260 / LXL 233 valid-EP legs), but their **mass placement** differs —
LXJ's spread into the empty mass gap (28–33 t between the 17 t unladen and 39 t laden
clusters), so they float in isolation and read as a distinct band; LXK/LXL's equivalent
legs land on the 17 t unladen line or inside the laden cluster and stay hidden.

**Fix** (in the `generate-pdf-report` skill — its own self-contained code, not routed to
jolt-toolkit-dev): added a **SOC-quantisation guard** `SOC_QUANT_GUARD_PCT = 1` in
`generate_pdf_report.py`. In `compute()`, right after the existing EP cleaning, any drive
leg with `|SOC Change| ≤ 1 %` has its **`ep` nulled** (same mechanism as EP cleaning) so
it drops out of the scatter / fit / conclusion stats — **but the leg still counts toward
page-1 distance/energy aggregates** (only the unreliable EP value is removed, not the
trip). Documented in SKILL.md §5 "Cleaning".

**Regenerated** all three trucks, both page-1 bases (raw + `_xlsxkpi` segment) at v2.2.6
`--all-data` → 6 PDFs. Guard dropped 923 / 260 / 233 EP values respectively.

**Verification**: AV24LXJ's EP-vs-GVM band is gone (fit intercept rises to a sensible
~1.0 at 10 t); the corresponding spurious high-range band on Range-vs-GVM also clears
(same legs inflated the reciprocal range). LXK/LXL unchanged apart from a slightly
thinner unladen low tail (no mid-mass band to begin with — no regression). All 6 PDFs
render as 2 A4 pages (595×842 pt), no clipping. AV24LXJ conclusions sane and monotone:
unladen 1.19 (±0.24) / laden 1.70 (±0.27) / full 1.79 (±0.24) kWh/km, +0.02 kWh/km per t,
−0.14 kWh/km per 10 °C colder. Skill code change not committed; artefacts gitignored.

## Q: Root-cause fix (v2.2.7) — `_correct_effective_capacity` step 2 was overwriting good counter energy on short trips, creating a spurious low-EP band

**Task**: The EP-vs-GVM briefing for AV24LXJ showed a spurious low band (≈ 0.7 kWh/km).
Root cause (pre-diagnosed): the post-segmentation `_correct_effective_capacity` **step 2**
(global ±1σ outlier correction) in `report_generator/_generator.py` back-calculated
`delta_energy = ΔSOC/100 × replacement_capacity` for **every** outlier row, including
counter-sourced legs. On a short trip, segmentation correctly takes ≈ 5.1 kWh from the
`total_electric_energy_used` counter, but the integer-% SOC channel logs a true ≈ 1.7 % drop
as 1 %, so the *implied* capacity = 5.146 / 0.01 ≈ 514 kWh is a physically impossible outlier.
Step 2 flagged 514 and overwrote the good counter energy with `0.01 × ~344 = 3.44 kWh`
(EP ≈ 0.70), inheriting the integer-SOC under-count. Concrete case: AV24LXJ 2024-06-27 19:32
In Transit (4.875 km) — counter Δ ≈ 5.146 kWh (true EP ≈ 1.06), but 2.2.6 booked 3.414 kWh /
EP 0.700.

**Result** (branch `fix/capacity-correction-counter-energy`, tag `v2.2.7`; **not pushed**):

- **Fix** in `_generator._correct_effective_capacity` step 2: gate the energy
  back-calculation on `energy_source`. The capacity column of every ±1σ outlier is still
  corrected (so the display value and the persisted effective-capacity average stay clean and
  unchanged), but `delta_energy` / `Energy Performance` / elevation-corrected / kinetics are

## Q: Complete the pending 2.2.7 Knowles regen; and (keeping MODE A for EP) make the effective-battery-capacity aggregation account for SOC-precision error — explore ΔSOC-weighting vs outlier removal

**Capacity-aggregation exploration.** Keeping MODE A (EP stays the counter value, never re-derived
from SOC), the remaining SOC-precision issue is in how the *effective capacity* is aggregated: the
per-donor implied capacity `C_i = |ΔE_i|/(|ΔSOC_i|/100)` is noisy AND upward-biased for small-ΔSOC
segments (σ_C/C ≈ 0.5/|ΔSOC|; Jensen bias on 1/ΔSOC). Verified on AV24LXJ **discharge** donors
(n=7707): ≤2% legs C̄=319 vs ≥15% legs C̄=291; **plain mean = 304.9** (inflated ~2.6%), median/±1σ/MAD
only reach ~300.5–300.9. Compared methods: **ΔSOC-weighted (w=ΔSOC) = 298.1** (near the 297 nominal),
ΔSOC≥5% cutoff = 297.8, ΔSOC≥10% cutoff = 287.4 (over-restricts), ΔSOC²/inverse-variance = 294.1
(over-weights the biggest legs, drifts low). Note the CHARGE donors (the *preferred* source) are all
large-ΔSOC (median 30 %) so charge-preferred capacity is already robust — this mainly fixes the
discharge-fallback path.

**Decision — (a) ΔSOC-weighted mean, w=|ΔSOC| (combined-ratio estimator** `C_eff = 100·Σ|ΔE|/Σ|ΔSOC|`
`= Σ(C_i·|ΔSOC_i|)/Σ|ΔSOC_i|`): uses all data (no arbitrary cutoff), smoothly down-weights the noisy
small-ΔSOC legs, and avoids the per-leg 1/small-ΔSOC blow-up entirely. Chosen over (b) a hard ΔSOC
cutoff (threshold-sensitive, discards data) and over ΔSOC² weighting (drifts low).
