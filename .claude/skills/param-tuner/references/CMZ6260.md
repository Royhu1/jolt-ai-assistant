## Parameter rationale

### `min_stop_duration_min = 15.0` (Round 2 raised 5 → 15)

- Delivery stops of 5-15 min (traffic lights, junctions, short interchanges) are absorbed, while long delivery stops (loading/unloading ≥ 15 min) are still split
- Natural rhythm of multi-drop city distribution: a raw trip plateau of 11-13 is the real state of the duty cycle, not forced down to 5 trips
- Round 2 measured: 380 → 341 driving legs (-39, -10.3%), with most Issue days dropping from 11-13 trips to 8-10 trips

### `trip_endpoint_anchor = zero_speed` + `max_extend_minutes = 5.0` (Round 2 enabled)

- Extends the trip endpoint to the nearest v == 0 telematics heartbeat point, avoiding the trip end landing on a momentary dip at 20-40 km/h
- Consistent with EX74JXW / EX74JXY (scania_speed_00) / similar configurations
- Round 1 Issue P2 (~20 cases endpoint v > 0) has been largely eliminated

### `merge_by_mass = false` (Round 1 setting retained)

- The 3-tier natural distribution of mass is stable (10 / 18-25 / 35-45 t), and the cluster split is accurate
- After merging, adjacent trips of the same cluster would be merged into a long In Transit, losing the multi-drop granularity
- Same strategy as KY24LHT / EX74JXW

### `min_cluster_gap_kg = 1500` (kept)

- The natural distribution of mass clusters has a gap > 5 t, so the 1500 threshold is healthy; no adjustment needed

## Lessons learned

1. **City multi-drop naturally has a higher trip count**: different from long-haul trunk such as KY24LHT (1-4 trips/day); do not force compression, otherwise the delivery granularity is lost.
2. **`min_stop=15` is a reasonable threshold for multi-drop distribution**: 5 min is too sensitive (cuts at traffic lights too), 30 min is too coarse (misses short loading/unloading). 15 min sits near the lower bound of loading/unloading time.
3. **The `zero_speed` anchor does not directly reduce the trip count, but markedly improves split quality**: aligning endpoints to v == 0 telematics heartbeats avoids EP computation being disturbed by a momentary speed dip.
4. **The doubled fig artefact (P3) is unrelated to parameter tuning**: it must be solved through FPS API leg dedup, which belongs to the `jolt-toolkit-dev` agent. The param-tuner report only counts the actual leg count in the xlsx.
5. **It is easy to misjudge doubled-fig days during R1 assessment**: when the two figures for the same day have different splits, both must be viewed together before issuing an "OK" verdict. R1 mislabelled 2026-02-16 as "2 trip OK" because it looked only at _0011 and missed _0054.
