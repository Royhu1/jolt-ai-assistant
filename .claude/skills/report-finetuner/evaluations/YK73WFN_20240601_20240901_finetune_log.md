### Known visual diffs vs originals

Per jolt-report-dev agent decision #7, finetuned figures **omit** two cosmetic layers:
- Panel 3 anchor triangles (▼▲ markers on energy-used curve)
- Panel 4 "Seg. Mean Mass" horizontal dashed lines

This is because those layers derive from `run_segment_detection`'s internal
`_anchor_*` / `mass_cluster` side-products, which are not reconstructable from
xlsx alone. Shading, dSOC labels, energy labels, speed curve, and SOC curve
are all preserved. For finetune purposes (verify post-edit segment positions)
this is sufficient.

## Process observations

### What the LLM vision reliably detects
- Idle days (flat SOC, zero speed, optional small charge counter noise)
- Clean single trips matching xlsx 1:1
- Multi-trip days: mass changes between trips confirm genuine delivery splits
- Charge events with matching SOC rise + green AC/DC delta panel
- Cargo loading/unloading events (mass step without speed activity)

### What needs xlsx cross-reference to decide
- Short Stop rows: duration + SOC drop only visible in xlsx numerics
- Post-charge SOC drop events: hard to distinguish aux drain vs DC rebalance
  vs missed short trip without raw CSV

### What I deliberately did NOT flag
- leg_type sub-classification (In Transit vs Outbound vs Return vs Round Trip) —
  label choice, not a segmentation error
- Minor charge counter noise (4-9 kWh increments when SOC is already at 100) —
  correctly filtered by `min_soc_rise`; creating a segment would be worse
- 2024-06-20's 8% SOC drop in a Stop — flagged but conservative-pass; needs raw
  CSV inspection to confirm root cause, risk of wrong fix outweighs benefit
